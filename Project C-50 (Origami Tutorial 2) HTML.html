Interesting Facts About Camels!

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  • Their humps let them store up to 80 pounds of fat which they can live off for weeks and even months!

    Interesting Facts About Swans!

  • They are huge! Trumpeter swans are the largest native waterfowl and the heaviest flying birds in North America.
  • Their elongated, curved necks and white feathers stand out amongst the lakes they reside on.
  • They are very large birds and can weigh up to 30 pounds, measuring anywhere from 56 – 62 inches in length.
  • A group of wild swans is known as a herd, however a group in captivity are called a fleet.

    Interesting Facts About Chameleons!

  • Chameleons or chamaeleons are a distinctive and highly specialized clade of Old World lizards with 202 species described as of June 2015.
  • These species come in a range of colors, and many species have the ability to change color.
  • On average, a chameleon's tongue is roughly twice the length of its body
  • In humans, that would be a tongue about 10 to 12 feet (about 3 to 4 meters) long.
